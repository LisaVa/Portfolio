Gowork

Designing and prototyping a location-based app that users to identify and compare coworking spaces in New York City


4 weeks | Concept Project


Research | Information Architecture | Visual Design | Prototyping

Background

Though the number of coworking spaces in New York City has grown exponentially in the past few years, there are surprisingly few resources to help potential users find the right coworking space for them. Freelancers and other potential coworkers may use standard crowd-sourced solutions such as Yelp, but these are not tailored to screen for specific worker needs. Similarly, while larger coworking solutions such as WeWork offer maps to identify their locations, this naturally limits the user to only one coworking option.


A map of coworking spaces in NYC, from The New Worker.


Given the success of platforms that help locate and compare space options in numerous other markets (for instance, Priceline in the travel market, Seamless in the restaurant market) I believed that offering a similar solution in the Coworking space will offer a needed solution to thousands of NYC based users.

Solution

This app represented the solution to a two sided problem. It needed both to represent what the coworking industry currently offers freelance/independent New Yorkers, and which of these many features people considered most important when choosing a workspace. With Gowork, users are able to easily search by location, filter by price and features, and read what other users have to say about their experience at locations. Moreover, with an account they can save spaces they are interested in, and write their own reviews to contribute to the broader community.
